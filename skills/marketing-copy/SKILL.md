# marketing-copy

Clear over clever. Specific over vague. Honest over hype. (Distilled in our own way from Corey Haines' marketing skills, MIT.) This governs the *words*, not the layout — pair with `anti-slop-frontend` / `redesign-existing-projects` for the page itself.

## 1. Positioning first (ask if missing — don't guess)
Before writing, pin these down (from the user, or the site/codebase):
one-liner · category ("what shelf you sit on") · ICP (company type + decision-maker role) · primary job-to-be-done · core problem + emotional tension · #1 differentiator · top-3 objections · **verbatim customer words** to use / to avoid.
Capture the customer's *exact phrases*, not polished descriptions.

## 2. The 6 principles (hard checklist)
1. Clarity over cleverness. 2. Benefits over features. 3. Specific over vague ("Cut reporting from 4hrs to 15min", not "save time"). 4. Customer language over company language. 5. One idea per section. 6. Honest over sensational.
Style: `use` not `utilize`; active not passive; confident not qualified (kill "almost / very / really"); show don't tell; **remove every exclamation point**; ban buzzwords (streamline / optimize / innovative / game-changing).

## 3. Always output options
For any headline or CTA, give **2–3 alternatives with rationale**. CTA formula: `[action verb] + [what they get] + [qualifier]` — never "Submit / Sign Up / Learn More" ("Start my free trial", "Get the checklist").

## 4. Frameworks (in the reference)
Headline formulas, the 11-section landing-page structure (+ compact & launch-post variants), the offer Value Equation, objection handling, the psychology bank, and the Seven Sweeps QA pass all live in [`references/copy-frameworks.md`](references/copy-frameworks.md). Read it when drafting.

## 5. QA before you ship
Run the **Seven Sweeps** (reference) and check the **banned-vocabulary** list. Every claim must be specific, attributed, and honest — specificity beats superlatives.
