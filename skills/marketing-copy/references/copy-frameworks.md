# Copy Frameworks

Reference for the `marketing-copy` skill. (Distilled from Corey Haines' marketing skills, MIT.)

## Headline formulas (give 2–3 options with rationale)
- **Outcome:** "{outcome} without {pain}" · "{outcome} by {how}" · "Turn {input} into {outcome}" · "{outcome} in {timeframe}"
- **Problem:** "Never {bad thing} again" · "{question about the pain}" · "Stop {pain}. Start {pleasure}."
- **Audience:** "{category} for {audience}" · "You don't have to {skill} to {outcome}"
- **Differentiation:** "The {category} that {differentiator}"
- **Proof:** "{N} {people} use {product} to {outcome}"
- **Other:** "Finally, {category} that {benefit}" · "The simple way to {outcome}" · "What if you could {outcome}?"

## CTA
`[action verb] + [what they get] + [qualifier]`. Ban weak CTAs (Submit / Sign Up / Learn More / Get Started). Use value-specific: "Start my free trial", "Get the complete checklist".

## Landing-page structure (a narrative, NOT a feature list)
Hero (headline + subhead + CTA + visual) → social-proof bar → problem/pain → how it works (3–4 numbered steps) → 2–3 key benefits (not 10) → testimonial → use cases/personas → comparison to alternatives → case-study snippet → FAQ (objections) → final CTA with risk reversal.
- **Compact (ad landing):** 6 sections. **Launch post:** what shipped → why it matters (the problem) → how it works → proof/example → CTA.
- Section roles: social proof = credibility · problem = recognition · solution = 3–5 benefits · how-it-works = reduce complexity · FAQ = objection handling · final CTA = recap + risk reversal.

## Offer > copy (Value Equation)
`Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)`. Score each lever 1–10; fix the lowest. A complete offer = core deliverable + bonus stack + guarantee/risk-reversal + REAL scarcity/urgency + a name + price/payment structure. A single honest change lifts 10–40%.

## Proof & objections
Testimonials need specific results ("increased conversions 32%"), before/after context, name + role + company, a photo when possible — reject "Great product!". Handle objections with a 5–10-question FAQ, comparison tables (vs competitors or vs the status quo), and risk reversal (money-back, free trial, cancel anytime). Put trust signals near the CTAs.

## Psychology bank (apply ethically; pick per problem)
Social proof · anchoring (show the high price first) · framing ("90% success" > "10% failure") · loss aversion · genuine scarcity · reciprocity (give value first) · authority ("featured in") · commitment/consistency (small ask first) · mental accounting ("$1/day" > "$30/mo") · paradox of choice (3 tiers, not 7) · open loops. Price objection → anchoring + framing + loss aversion.

## Seven Sweeps (QA — one pass each, loop back)
1. Clarity. 2. Voice & tone. 3. So-what (feature → benefit). 4. Prove it (substantiate claims, attribute proof, no unearned superlatives). 5. Specificity (numbers, timeframes). 6. Heightened emotion. 7. Zero risk (objections handled near the CTA, risk reversal stated).

## Banned vocabulary
"game-changing / revolutionary / disruptive / next-level / 10x" (AI-slop) · "secret / hidden / what they don't want you to know" (clickbait) · "limited time" with no real deadline (lying) · "worth $X value" with no comparable · "100% guaranteed" with no conditions. Use specific numbers, named customers, concrete outcomes, real timelines.
